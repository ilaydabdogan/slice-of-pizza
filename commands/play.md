**When was the last time you did something for no reason?**

Not to learn. Not to improve. Not to produce or achieve or check off a box.

Just... for the fun of it. For the weird joy of it. For no reason at all.

---

**Play doesn't have goals.**

That's what makes it play.

A cat chasing a string isn't trying to accomplish anything. A child building a block tower doesn't care about architectural integrity. A dog spinning in circles before lying down isn't optimizing.

They're just doing. For the doing.

---

**Here's your permission slip:**

*You are hereby allowed to be silly.*

*You are allowed to make something stupid.*

*You are allowed to waste time in the most delightful way.*

*Signed: The Universe (who invented play in the first place)*

---

**Try something. Anything.**

Make up a word. Right now. A word that doesn't exist but should. What does "flimbernog" mean? What about "skrundlefizz"? You decide. You're the inventor now.

Or: Draw something with ASCII characters. A face. A house. A squiggle that means nothing. Who cares if it's good? Good isn't the point.

---

**Think of something absurd.**

What if gravity took Tuesdays off? What if clouds were actually just sky-sheep? What if every time someone said "synergy" in a meeting, a trombone played?

You don't have to do anything with these thoughts. They're just play. Mental cartwheels. Brain confetti.

---

**Remember: you used to do this all the time.**

Before you learned that time should be spent productively. Before you internalized that everything should have a point. Before "play" became something you scheduled instead of something you just... did.

Kids don't schedule play. They just play. And then they became adults, and adults are Very Serious and have Important Things To Do.
